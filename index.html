Post-Occupy surge in voters during Hong Kong’s 2016 Legco elections

Hong Kong political engagment in Lego election continue to rise

By Jessie Pang


According to the latest statistics in the data.gov.hk, there are now 3,779,085 registered electors in geographical constituencies, a net increase of 312,884 electors since the last Legco lection in 2012.

The Legco elections were the first since the pro-democracy Occupy protests in 2014, and returned six young lawmakers advocating self-determination for Hong Kong, including student activist Nathan Law Kwun-chung.

Among the registered electors in geographical constituencies, New Territories West has the highest no. of registered electors (1,086,511). Meanwhile, female voters slightly outnumber male voters by 3.8%.

Although there was a decrease of registered electors in functional constituencies, the total no. gradually rise back to 239,724 as the election approached. Among the registered electors in functional constituencies,education sector has the highest no. of voters (88,185). The sector’s lawmaker said that although this may be due to vote-rigging activities, he believed the increase in the number of voters was still a good thing.

Political scientist Dr Chung Kim-wah, of Polytechnic University’s Centre for Social Policy Studies, said Hong Kong people political engagment in election has risen as they have a stronger political indenity since the Occupy Movement in 2014.

“They now care about politics; want to devote themselves to social movements; and want to change the game rules,” Chung said. “But it remains to be seen whether their passion, and the trend [of the rising turnout], can be sustained.”
